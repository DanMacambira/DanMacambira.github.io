I am a MRes student in Economics at the <a href="https://www.parisschoolofeconomics.eu/en/about/">Paris School of Economics (PSE)</a> working under the supervision of Jérôme Bourdieu on the financing of primary public schools in late nineteenth century France. Prior to that, I completed a MPhil dissertation, under the supervision of Alexis Litvine, on "The public provision of education: investments, governance and school quality in the Pas-de-Calais during the early Third Republic" at [Cambridge](https://www.hist.cam.ac.uk), Queens' College. I also worked as a Pre-Doc on American Public Health, with Chima Ndumele and Jacob Wallace, at the Yale School of Public Health as part of the Tobin predoctoral program at [Yale](https://tobin.yale.edu/programs/pre-doctoral-fellows-program).

My main interest is the construction of the nation-state through the provision of public services. I am currently working on the provision of primary education but I am also very much interested in the organisation of health care and health insurance in the US and in Europe.
